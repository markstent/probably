Gamma distribution
X ~ Gamma(α, β)
The standard prior for rates and precisions. If your parameter must be positive and represents how fast something happens, Gamma is the natural choice.

As a prior
Prior on rates and precisions
Gamma is the conjugate prior for the rate parameter of Poisson and Exponential likelihoods, and for the precision (1/σ²) of a Normal. Use it when your parameter is strictly positive and represents a rate or inverse-variance. The parametrisation matters: some software uses shape and scale (θ = 1/β); always check which convention your tools use.
Gamma(ε, ε) for small ε can cause pathological behaviour — prefer half-Normal on σ instead.
Gamma(α, α) with large α concentrates around 1, useful for a rate you expect near 1.
Exponential(λ) is Gamma(1, λ) — the memoryless special case.
As a likelihood
Likelihood for positive continuous data
Gamma is a flexible likelihood for strictly positive, right-skewed continuous data: rainfall amounts, hospital stay durations, insurance claim sizes, response times. Its shape parameter controls skewness — small α gives high right-skew, large α approaches symmetric. The Exponential (α = 1) is the memoryless special case.
Compare to Log-Normal: both model right-skewed positives but Log-Normal has a heavier right tail.
If data has a point mass at zero, consider a zero-inflated model with a Gamma component.

Prior: λ ~ Gamma(α, β) Likelihood: xᵢ ~ Poisson(λ), i = 1…n Posterior: λ | x ~ Gamma(α + Σxᵢ, β + n) Posterior mean = (α + Σxᵢ) / (β + n) → weighted average of prior mean α/β and MLE Σxᵢ/n
For Exponential likelihood: Gamma(α + n, β + Σxᵢ) — the roles of n and Σxᵢ swap.
Worked examples
Event rate in a Poisson process (e.g. server requests per second): Gamma(2, 0.1) prior on λ, updated as request counts accumulate.
Precision (1/σ²) in a Normal model: Gamma prior gives Normal-Gamma joint conjugacy, the foundation of conjugate Bayesian linear regression.
Insurance claim frequency: λ ~ Gamma(3, 1) prior updated as claims arrive, posterior gives credibility-weighted rate estimate.
Waiting time modelling: Gamma(2, 1) encodes "at least one thing must happen before the event" — the Erlang special case.
